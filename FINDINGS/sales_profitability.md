# Sales and Profitability by Department and Category

## Business Question
Which business units and product categories drive the most profit?

## Analysis Approach
I used SQL to aggregate total benefit, average profit ratio, and 
total orders grouped by department and category to identify the 
most valuable units in the business.

## Department Level Finding
Fan Shop leads with approximately 1.8 million in total benefit at 
a 12% profit ratio. While Technology, Outdoors, and Fitness show 
a slightly higher profit ratio at 13%, their combined total 
benefit is less than one third of Fan Shop's — making the 
difference negligible in absolute terms.

| Department | Total Benefit | Profit Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Fan Shop | ~1.8M | 12% |
| Technology | ~113K | 13% |
| Outdoors | ~145K | 13% |
| Fitness | ~46K | 13% |

## Category Level Finding — Three Tiers Emerged

**Tier One — Proven Revenue Engine (11-13% profit ratio)**
High volume categories that sustain the business — Fishing, Cleats, 
Cardio Equipment, Camping and Hiking, and Women's Apparel.

**Tier Two — Efficient but Underscaled (14-15% profit ratio)**
Women's Clothing, Garden, and Music — higher efficiency per sale 
with room to grow volume.

**Tier Three — Biggest Untapped Opportunity (16-19% profit ratio)**
Golf Bags, Cars, and Toys — highest profit ratios but lowest 
volumes in the portfolio.

## Recommendation
Sustain investment in Tier One given its proven volume and 
consistent profit contribution. Prioritise Tier Two for scaling 
given its higher efficiency and demonstrated demand. Deploy 
targeted marketing and pricing strategies to unlock Tier Three's 
full potential.
